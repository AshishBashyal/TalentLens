# TalentLens Project Vision

## Overview

TalentLens is an AI-powered Job Market Intelligence Platform that transforms job posting data into actionable labor market insights.

Modern job markets change quickly. Skills rise and decline, salary expectations shift, hiring demand moves across locations, and new technologies reshape roles faster than traditional reports can track. TalentLens aims to make those changes visible through data engineering, analytics, natural language processing, machine learning, and business intelligence.

The long-term ambition is to evolve TalentLens from a portfolio-grade project into a credible business-grade analytics platform.

## Problem Statement

Students, job seekers, recruiters, and businesses often make decisions using fragmented or outdated job market information.

Common challenges include:

- Students do not know which skills are most valuable for their target roles.
- Job seekers struggle to benchmark salaries and identify hiring hotspots.
- Recruiters lack visibility into market-wide skill shortages.
- Businesses need workforce planning signals but rely on slow reports or manual research.
- Job descriptions contain valuable information, but much of it is unstructured text.

TalentLens addresses these problems by collecting job posting data, structuring it, extracting meaning from descriptions, and generating clear insights.

## Product Mission

TalentLens helps people and organizations make smarter career, hiring, and workforce decisions using labor market data.

## Product Principles

### Data First

TalentLens should be built around clean, traceable, and reliable data pipelines. Analytics and ML features are only as strong as the data foundation beneath them.

### Incremental Intelligence

The platform will not jump directly into advanced AI. It will first establish reliable collection, storage, cleaning, and analytics. NLP and machine learning will be introduced in later phases.

### Explainable Insights

Insights should be understandable. Users should know what trend is being shown, where it came from, and how it can support a decision.

### Business-Grade Architecture

The system should be organized like a real platform, with clear boundaries between data collection, processing, storage, APIs, reporting, and ML.

### Ethical and Responsible Collection

Data collection should respect source terms, rate limits, robots policies, and legal constraints. The platform should prioritize public, permitted, and responsibly accessed data.

## Target Users

### Students

Students use TalentLens to identify high-value skills, understand industry demand, and choose learning paths aligned with market needs.

### Job Seekers

Job seekers use TalentLens to compare salary expectations, evaluate locations, understand experience requirements, and identify emerging roles.

### Recruiters

Recruiters use TalentLens to track hiring competition, skill shortages, role demand, and market movement.

### Businesses

Businesses use TalentLens to support workforce planning, technology adoption analysis, and labor market monitoring.

## Core Insight Areas

TalentLens will eventually provide:

- Skill demand trends.
- Salary trends.
- Technology adoption trends.
- Location-based hiring trends.
- Company hiring activity.
- Experience requirement patterns.
- Emerging and declining skills.
- Role clustering.
- Career recommendations.
- Skill-gap analysis.
- Market intelligence reports.

## Long-Term Platform Direction

TalentLens can evolve into:

- A self-service analytics platform for labor market intelligence.
- A reporting engine for students, universities, recruiters, and businesses.
- A recommendation system for career planning.
- A data product that tracks skill and technology demand over time.
- A foundation for advanced AI features such as forecasting and personalized career paths.

## Definition of Success

TalentLens is successful when it can:

- Collect job data reliably from multiple sources.
- Convert unstructured descriptions into structured intelligence.
- Show useful trends through analytics and reports.
- Support decision-making for different user groups.
- Demonstrate strong software architecture and data engineering practice.
- Grow from a project into a platform without requiring major rewrites.
